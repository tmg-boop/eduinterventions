What Is West Virginia's Hope Scholarship?

The West Virginia Hope Scholarship is an Education Savings Account (ESA) program managed by the West Virginia State Treasurer's Office. Instead of your child's education funding going to a public school, the state deposits it directly into a parent-controlled scholarship account. Families can then use those funds for approved educational expenses — including tutoring from certified educators like those at Education Interventions.

The scholarship amount equals the statewide average per-pupil net state aid from the prior year — $5,267.38 for 2025–26, with the projected amount rising to approximately $5,435.62 for 2026–27. Funds are distributed in two installments: half by August 15 and half by January 15. Unused funds roll over to the following school year.

A major advantage for tutoring families: There is no cap on how much of your Hope Scholarship can go toward tutoring. Homeschool families who are not paying private school tuition can direct their entire annual award toward expert literacy tutoring — covering a full year of weekly sessions at no out-of-pocket cost.

2026–27 Universal Expansion — Big News for WV Families

Starting with the 2026–2027 school year, West Virginia's Hope Scholarship expands to true universal eligibility. Previously, students needed to have attended a West Virginia public school for at least 45 consecutive days to qualify. That requirement is being removed entirely — meaning private school students, homeschoolers, and public school students can all apply without first transferring to public school.

If you previously didn't qualify because your child was in private school or homeschooled, the 2026–27 expansion changes that. Applications for 2026–27 typically open in March — get ready to apply.

Who Currently Qualifies?

For the 2025–2026 school year, students must be West Virginia residents and meet one of these criteria: be eligible to enroll in kindergarten for the current school year, have attended a West Virginia public school for the entire previous instructional term, or have attended a West Virginia public school for at least 45 consecutive calendar days during the current term and remain enrolled until application approval.

For the 2026–2027 school year, the attendance requirement is removed entirely — all K–12 West Virginia residents become eligible.

How to Apply — Step by Step

1

Visit the Official Hope Scholarship Portal

Go to hopescholarshipwv.gov and create an account. This is the official State Treasurer's Office portal where all applications, payments, and account management happen.

2

Gather Required Documents

You'll need proof of West Virginia residency (driver's license, utility bills, or tax records), your child's WVEIS ID number (West Virginia student ID — request from your county board of education if your child has never attended public school), and documentation of eligibility.

3

Submit Your Application

Applications typically open in March with a deadline around June 15. Apply as early as possible — funding is prorated based on when you apply. Families who apply after June 15 receive reduced amounts (75%, 50%, or 25% depending on timing).

4

Submit Notice of Intent

Once your application shows "Eligible," submit a Notice of Intent (NOI) to your county board of education. This officially withdraws your student from public school if applicable and notifies the county of your participation.

5

Link Your Bank Account and Receive Funds

West Virginia uses Plaid-connected bank accounts for fund distribution. Link your account through the portal. The first disbursement (half your annual award) arrives by August 15, the second by January 15.

6

Select Education Interventions and Start Tutoring

Browse the Hope Scholarship marketplace for approved tutoring services. Education Interventions is an approved vendor. Contact us to get started — all purchases are made through the portal and paid directly to us. No out-of-pocket cost for your family.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do unused Hope Scholarship funds roll over?

Yes — unused funds roll over to the following school year and remain available for approved expenses. This is a significant advantage over programs like Arkansas's EFA where unused funds expire at year end.

Can my child with a learning disability use the Hope Scholarship?

Yes. Students with disabilities can participate in the Hope Scholarship. However, participation is considered a parental placement under IDEA — meaning the public school system is released from its obligation to provide Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) while your child participates. Discuss this carefully with your family before applying if your child currently receives public school special education services.

My child has never attended public school. Can they get a WVEIS ID?

Yes — if your child has never attended a West Virginia public school, you can request a WVEIS ID from your county board of education. This is required for the application. Contact your local county board office to initiate the process.

Can I use Hope Scholarship funds for virtual tutoring?

Absolutely. Online tutoring from approved vendors is a fully qualifying expense. Education Interventions provides 100% virtual sessions making expert support accessible from anywhere in West Virginia.

What if I apply after June 15?

Your award will be prorated based on your application date — 75% if you apply by September 15, 50% by November 30, or 25% by February 28. Apply as early as possible to receive the full annual award.
